      <br> &nbsp;&nbsp; <em>PhD, Bioinformatics and Computational Biology</em>
      <br> &nbsp;&nbsp; <u>Image processing and analysis methods in quantitative endothelial cell biology</u>
      <ul>
        <li>The thesis presents the development of computerised image processing and analysis pipelines for microscopy image data in endothelial vascular biology experimentation.</li>
        <li>The objective of the work is to advance understanding of the cell biology of cardiovascular processes, including haemostasis, thrombosis, and inflammation.</li>
        <li>Bioinformatics techniques are necessary for extracting and evaluating information from biological experimentation.</li>
        <li>Three computerised workflows are described for analysing microscopy images in cardiovascular cell biology:
          <ul>
            <li>The first workflow segments cell organelles and extracts morphometric features from high-throughput experiments imaging endothelial cells and organelles to investigate haemostatic mechanisms.</li>
            <li>The second workflow uses super-resolution microscopy to differentiate between healthy volunteers and patients with Hermansky-Pudlak syndrome by segmenting and counting the number of CD63-positive structures per platelet.</li>
            <li>The third workflow is a video analysis method that quantifies interactions of leukocytes with an endothelial monolayer using object detection and custom tracking.</li>
          </ul>
        </li>
        <li>These methodologies provide a toolkit to further biological understanding of multiple facets of cardiovascular behavior.</li>
      </ul>
